Preparing for the Draft

Planning for the draft makes the difference between having a mediocre team or having a team that can go the distance and win a Super Bowl. Taking the time to research players, their teams, schedules, bye weeks and stats will give you the upper hand in drafting NFL players. There are many different types of Fantasy Football magazines available at your local book store, TV talk shows and the internet.  These are just a few ways to help you prepare and achieve the winning edge over your opponents.

Division Setup 
Depending on the number of owners, division alignments will be as follows:
  1. Up to 72 owner league.
  2. One conference with up to 9 divisions.
  3. 8 owners per division (10 owners maximum).

The League will do it's best to accommodate you by division with owners you would enjoy playing against. Hopefully this will make for a more enjoyable season.

Day of Draft

Before the draft starts we will go over this year's rules and answer any questions. Every owner should come prepared with a list of NFL players at each position so that he or she can keep track of who has been drafted and which players are still available to select from. Owners will draft their players in proper order taking approximately one minute per pick. The average time to complete a draft is about two to three hours long.

Setting up draft order

Each owner in their respective divisions, by a show of hands, will vote on a draft format shown below.  In case of a tie, draft type will be drawn from a hat or by other means.  Each draft type is explained below and all owners should be familiarized with each style prior to draft day; as follows:

  • Reverse - Every other round is reserve to the prior round, i.e., round 1 = 1-8, round 2 = 8-1, round 3 = 1-8, and etc.
  • Random - each owner draws a number from a deck of cards, one thru the number of owners in a division. This number will become the position in which an owner will draft in the first round and then it becomes your assigned position throughout the remaining rounds.  To help all owners drafting strategy, all 16 rounds will be determine before hand.
  • Serpentine - a combine format of Random All and Reverse.  All odd rounds are randomized with the even rounds in reverse order.
    Example:  round 1 = 1-8, round 2 = 8-1, round 3 = 4-3, round 4 = 3-4, round 5 = 6-5, round 6 = 5-6, and etc.

Note: if a draft format cannot be derived, Reverse type is league's default format.

Team Roster

One important key in fantasy football is not to draft too many players on the same bye week. If you were to draft a starting QB from the New England and your second QB from the Baltimore Ravens, each having the same bye week, you would score zero points for the position. Another scenario many owners tend to make is drafting to may players from same bye weeks, which weakens your changes of winning your match up.  Mixing your roster with players throughout the thirty-two NFL teams will help to eliminate the possibilities of this situation from occurring.

Everybody knows that injuries, benching, suspensions and holdouts are part of the game, so naturally they're part of this one, too. Throughout the season some owners won't have the ability to field a starting line-up each week. The following rulings will be used to determine a player(s) Add/Drop eligibility.

Notes:
  • Reasses your waiver move as decision is final when processed!
  • If emailed, request must be received prior to kickoff of the final game for the current week.
  • If waiver limit of 2 per week has been reached for the current week, no waiver can be made.
  • Waiver move will count towards season total of 17.

Placed on season ending Injured Reserve (IR)

Players placed on season ending IR can be released and replaced at any time; including playoffs, with a player from the free agent pool (see weekly Add/Drop for details).

Notes:
  • Out for the season is defined as player's expected return date is after week 17 (12/30/2025) is complete.
  • Any owners with season ending IR player(s) will be processed first before the weekly Add/Drop's are processed.

Placed on "Short Term" Injured Reserve (IR)

Players placed on Short Term IR (not season ending) cannot be released and replaced at any time, including playoffs. These players normally are out for a minimum of 3 weeks.
Note: At the owners discretion, these players can be replaced via the Add/Drop and will count towards season total moves one can make.

Injured during season but not on season ending Injured Reserve (IR)

Players listed out for a certain number of weeks for a particular injury (except for season ending IR. see above) will not qualify to be waived during any part of the season including the leagues's playoffs. You will have to retain that injured player on your roster or use the Add/Drop to release and select another player from the free agent pool. No moves will be conducted during the playoffs (except for season ending IR, released, or retired), so the depth of your team could be the deciding factor in winning the Super Bowl Championship!

Player retires in mid-season

Players that opt to retire in mid-season can be released and replaced at any time, including playoffs, with a player from the free agent pool (see weekly Add/Drop for details).
Move will not count against season total.

Note: Player is not eligible for First Come First Serve (FCFS) like an season ending IR.  One will have to use the weekly Add/Drop option.

Conflicts with players on a bye week or another player injury

When one or more players are on a bye and your injured player(s) can not start that week, the following guidelines for each position will determine if you can use a free agent player for current week.

Quarterbacks (TMQB) & Team Place Kickers (TMPK)

The team quarterback (TMQB) and team kicker (TMPK) positions are not be affected by player injuries.  The team concept groups all listed quarterbacks and kickers into this position.  

Example: Lets say you draft Patrick Mahomes as to drafting Kansas City TMQB.  If Mahomes gets injured, his backup game stats will not tabulate into fantasy points and your QB position would no longer incur points.  The value of the team concept gives your team the ability to get a complete game at the QB and PK when a injury occurs during the game.

Running Backs and Wide Receivers/Tight Ends

If you are unable to play the required number of Running Backs and Wide Receivers/Tight Ends on your roster, you will have to pick up an available player from the free agent pool during the weekly Add/Drop or during the FCFS moves (see weekly Add/Drop for more details).

Important notes:
  1. There are NO one week player substitutions for any of these positions.
  2. Player who comes down with an illnesses (e.g. Covid-19).
  3. If you are unable to field a full starting roster because of having to many players on the same bye week, you will not be allowed to pick up a free agent player after the Add/Drop deadlines have passed; and
  4. All rulings will be made by the League Commissioner&';s and are final. We will be as fair to everyone as possible. If you are still unclear about these rulings, please e-mail us.

Player released by NFL team

Players that are released during mid-season can be replaced with a player from the free agent pool (see weekly Add/Drop for more details).  Add/Drop moves will not count against season total.

Note: Player is not eligible for First Come First Serve (FCFS) like season ending IR.  One will have to use the weekly Add/Drop to make a move.

Suspended, Benched or Holdout Players

When one or more players are suspended for discipline actions, benched by their NFL team, or holds out due to contract disputes; these action apply to your roster as well.  These players WILL NOT be granted any special circumstances and should be handle wisely by you.

WARNING: Suspended, benched, or holdout players cannot be dropped during the leagues playoffs.

Weekly Add/Drop

Weekly Add/Drop will be held throughout the first twelve weeks of the regular season and first two rounds of the playoffs.  There will be no Add/Drops being during the final three weeks of the league's playoffs except for season ending IR, released or retired players.  Each owner will have a total of 17 Add/Drop moves until ceiling is met.

Notes:
  1. During the regular season, a maximum of 2 Add/Drop moves are allowed per week.
  2. During the first two rounds of league playoffs, a single Add/Drop move is allowed per week.
  3. Season ending IR, released, and retired players including TMQB & TMPK do not count against season Add/Drop total.

Add/Drop Requests

Add/Drop Weekly Request
Starting with the final game on Monday at 8:15 pm until Thurday 12:00 am, owners can begin submitting via league's web site or by emailing the League Commish, Add/Drop requests for players an owner would like to release and draft. Add/Drop's should contain at least 3 players of choice per released player. Each player drafted must be of the same position as the player released (exception would be RB for WR/TE or TMPK, WR/TE for RB or TMPK, or TMPK for WR/TE or RB). This should guarantee you at least one player from the free agent pool.

Note: if web site is down, all Add/Drop requests should be sent to UFFL2k@aol.com

After the deadline has passed, the League will compile all the Add/Drop requests each week and start the draft in the following order.

  • Owners with season ending IR requests (must be noted in the comment area) will be manually processed first if submitted by the weekly Add/Drop request deadline.  If more than one owner is making a request for the same NFL player, division standing (last to first) will be used to awarded the replacement player.  If season ending IR request are sent via email, time of email will be the deciding factor if both players went on season ending IR on the same day.  Otherwise, email will follow Add/Drop rules.
  • Two (2) rounds will be conducted each week during the regular season. Team in last place from each respective division will have the first pick and first place having the last pick. Second round will follow first round draft order.
  • One (1) round will be conducted for the first two rounds of league's playoffs. Week 12 final standing will determine Add/Drop order for the two rounds of the playoffs; last place team from each respective division will have the first pick and first place team having the last pick.
  • During the league's playoff (weeks 15 thru 17), Add/Drop is open for season ending IR, released, or retired players moves ONLY!
    Note: TMQB and TMPK position are eligible for season ending IR moves during weeks 15 thru 17.
  • In case of same record, record in head-to-head matchup and total points scored to date will determine the tiebreaker; day and time sent will be the forth tiebreaker, if needed.
  • Date or time of Add/Drop request has no impact on where you draft, except for season ending IR players. So, don't rush you selections.

Not all owners will draft players in a given week. The outcome of this will not change the order of the draft. For example, if the first top five owners (1-5) submitted a free agent requests and the last five teams (6-8) do not, the draft would start with the fifth place team going first.

Live Add/Drop - First Come First Serve (FCFS)
Starting each Thursday at 6:00 am; exceptions noted below, until Monday's night kickoff, owners can make real time moves on a FCFS basis.  League season and weekly Add/Drop maximums apply, but it is possible to make one Add/Drop request and then one live FCFS move in a given week. Player(s) released by owners during the weekly Add/Drop request will not be available until the following week. This will allow owners ample time to review the transaction page to see who was dropped and picked via the Add/Drop request.

Important dates and times, as follows:

  • Add/Drop requests opens with the NFL Kickoff game on Thursday September 4th, 2025 at 8:15 pm to Monday September 8th at 8:15 pm. 
  • Weekly live Add/Drop (FCFS) starts Thursday at 6:00 am to Monday 8:15 pm beginning with week 2.
  • NFL players playing on Thursday night will be locked out at start of their game, normally 8:20 pm and remain locked to Monday 9:00 pm.
  • Week 14, final live Add/Drop (FCFS) ends for the season.  Requests end Monday December 5th at 8:15 pm.
  • Weeks 15 thru 17, live Add/Drop (FCFS) is closed during the final 3 weeks of league playoffs.
Live Add/Drop Moves - Preseason
Once your drafted players are posted on the league's site, an owner can make unlimited preseason Add/Drop moves until NFL Kickoff Game on Thursday September 4th, 2025 at 8:15 pm.  All Add/Drop's are on FCFS basis. Each player or team positions (TMQB, TMPK & Defense) pickup must be of the same position as the player released.  The exception would be the flex position where you can waive a RB for WR/TE or TMPK, WR/TE for RB or TMPK, or TMPK for RB or WR/TE).  Add/Drop's processed during the preseason do not count towards season Add/Drop total.

Warning: Email responses during the preseason will not be except, Add/Drop must be initiated via the league's web site!

Playoff Formats

Week 13 thru 17 (5 weeks) will the designated as playoffs weeks.  League's 27 team tournament bracket can be viewed here.

League tournaments as follows:

Super Bowl (27 teams):

  • The top 3 teams from each division advance to the Super Bowl.
  • Each division winner will be seeded 1 - 9.  Rest will be seeded 10 - 27 by win/loss and season total points accumulated thru the regular season.
  • The top 5 #1 seeds will be rewarded a first round bye.
    Note: a starting line is still required to accumulate a weekly score in bye week.
  • Reseeding will incur in each round.  Highest remaining seed plays lowest seed remaining, 2nd highest seed played 2nd lowest seed, and etc.
  • As teams are knocked out from the Super Bowl first 2 rounds, he or she will get an automatic bid to the league's Consolation Bowl with his or total points to date being ranked among the other teams.
  • Teams knocked out from the 3rd & 4th rounds will advance to the Redemption Tournament.
  • Tournament to reward 2 prizes (1st & 2nd place).  See point distributions for breakdown.

2nd Chance and Redemption Tournaments (6 teams):

  • Format to remain head-to-head.
  • The 4 teams knocked out from the Super Bowl 3rd round will be reseeded into the Redemption Tournament with winners advancing to play for 5th & 6th place.
  • The 2 teams knocked out from the Super Bowl 4th round play for 3rd & 4th place.
  • The 2 teams knocked out from the Redemption Tournament 4th round will advance to the Consolation Bowl with his or her total points to date being ranked among the other teams.
  • Tournaments to reward 4 prizes (3rd, 4th, 5th & 6th places).  See point distributions for breakdown.

Consolation Bowl (30 teams):

  • Format changes from head-to-head to total points scoring system for 5 weeks.
  • All 4 place teams earn a wild card birth to the Consolation Bowl (9 teams).
  • Teams knocked out from the Super Bowl first 2 rounds and from 2nd Chance Bowl 4th round will advance here with his or her total points to date being ranked among the other teams.
  • Tournament to reward top 4 scoring teams (7th, 8th, 9th & 10th places).  See point distributions for breakdown.

Toilet Bowl (36 teams):

  • Format changes from head-to-head to total points scoring system for 5 weeks.
  • Teams not participating in the Super Bowl or Consolation Bowl advance here.
  • No automatic bids from the Super Bowl or Consolation Bowl will not advance here.
  • Tournament to reward top 4 scoring teams (11th, 12th 13th & 14th places).  See point distributions for breakdown.

Resolving tie match up during playoffs
If a head-to-head match end in a tie, your 9th player; starter or bench, will determine overtime. If your 9th player results in a tie, the following below will be used to determine a winner.

  1. The next highest scoring player from each team's bench will be used until a winner can be determine.
  2. If all bench players have been exhausted, and teams are from same division, regular season standing tie-break will be used.  See regular season tie-breaker above.
  3. If teams are from different divisions, teams regular season record will be used with total points as a tie-breaker if teams have same record.
  4. Coin flip.

Pick'em Pool

What is a Pick'em Pool? The general concept for this pool is to correctly pick the winners of each game each week of the NFL regular season. Each correct team you select scores you a win (ties are consider a loss). The owner with the most wins at the end of week 18 is declared the winner.

Notes:
  1. Pool incurs additional fee of $25 to play.
  2. Distribution and number of top finishes will be based on the amount of owners participating.
  3. All pool picks are hidden from view until kickoff of each game. This prevents one from seeing other owners selections.  
  4. Picks that have been submitted and where games have not been started are displayed as an asterisk (*).
  5. Pool concludes at the end of the NFL regular season, which is week 18.

Survivor Pool

What is a Survivor Pool? Each owner picks a winner from one NFL game for the upcoming week of the season. If one picks correctly and has less than 2 losses, he or she moves on and  picks in the following week. If you pick incorrectly twice, you're out and cannot pick in any subsequent weeks. The twist, an owner may only pick an NFL team once for the entire season. The last owner left is declared the winner. Distribution is based on the amount of owner involved.

Notes:
  1. Pool incurs additional fee of $25 to play.
  2. Distribution will be based on the amount of owners participating.
  3. Your pick can be submitted until the kickoff of the last game of the week.
  4. All pool picks are hidden from view until kickoff of each game. This prevents one from seeing each others selection.
  5. Owners selection that have been submitted and where the games have not been started are displayed as an asterisk (*).
  6. Incorrect pick, no selection made or game ends in a tie is viewed as a lossing pick.
  7. Owner is eliminated from the pool with 2nd loss.
  8. If a winner cannot be determine at the end of week 18, distribution will be divided among owners who have the least amount of losses.
    Ex: After week 18 concludes and there are 2 owners with 0 losses and 3 with 1 loss; distribution will be split among the 2 owners with 0 losses.
