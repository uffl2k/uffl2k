Ultimate Fantasy Football League
Super Bowl Challenge Rules and Scoring

Objective:
 Create a fantasy roster made up from players involved in the NFL Playoffs. Owner with the most cumulative total points throughout the Post Season, including the Super Bowl, will be declared the winner.

Roster Requirements:
 Each fantasy roster must consist of nine (9) total players. Roster requirements as follows,

Playoff Roster
1 - Team Quarterback 1 to 3 - Running Backs (see flex)
1 - Team Place Kicker 3 to 5 - Wide Receivers / Tight Ends (see flex)
1 - Team Defense/Special Teams NOTE: Rb & Wr/Te must add up to a total of 6 players.

Flex Position (Rb & We/Te):
Allows an owner to start a more flexible lineup at Running Back and Wide Receiver/Tight End. Each position has a minimum and a maximum requirement; one RB and three WR/TE is the league's default starters. This leaves two players to float to capitalize on key game match ups. One of three possible combinations can be used, as follows:

Possible Flex Position Combinations
1 Rb & 5 Wr's/Te's 2 Rb's & 4 Wr's/Te's  3 Rb's & 3 Wr's/Te's

Team Quarterback (TMQB) & Team Place Kicker (TMPK):
When you draft a quarterback, lets say Patrick Mahomes, you will also draft all his backups. When Peyton gets injured or benched, you will still accumulate fantasy stats on his backup(s). This will prevent you from not getting a complete game at the Qb position.

Note:  
Team Qb stats consists of added all Qb's that played and scored as a unit. Adjustments, if any, can be view from the Week Summary Report (live scoring doesn't show any adjustments of this type).

Team Place Kicker follows the same ruling as the Team Quarterback.

Drafting:
On or before the any game begins, a entry form must be submitted to the league before the first round of the playoffs. Two methods will be honored, as follows:

1. Submitting an email with your full name, team alias, address, city/state/zip, phone #, a password to grant access to site,
    and a team roster(s), or
2. Using the submission form either from the league's web site or from your email.

When form or email is received, your team page will be setup (by the start of first playoff game) and a link will be emailed in where you can access the site.

Waivers:
 There is no limit in how many waivers an owner can make from week to week. The objective is to score the most cumulative total points throughout the playoffs by selecting the best players each week.

Notes:
1. Injured players or players on a losing teams should be traded for players advancing to the next round if you don't want to score zero points.
2. When waivers are performed weekly, each player waived must be of the same position as the player dropped. The exception would 
    be waiving a Running Back for a Wide Receiver/Tight End or vise versa (see roster requirements).
3. Owners failing to stay within the roster requirements will be penalized 100 points in the current week where illegal waiver(s) were made.

Scoring:
 The following Scoring Grids will translate on field performance into fantasy points. Offensive players can score points in any of the other categories depending on their on field performance. Example: Wr, Te, Pk, or Qb rushes for a touchdown, Rb, Wr, Te, or Pk throws a touchdown pass, & so on.

Submitting Lineups and Initiating Waivers:
 Once a owner has been setup with a team page, all waivers and lineups can be made possible from the league's web site in real-time. Deadline to decide players waivers is prior to the start of each NFL game; see chart below for actual deadline times.  Owners will be responsible for making their own waivers and submitting lineup.

WARNING!!! Once a game is in progress, you no longer can start or waiver a player from that game. However, a waiver is possible for a player(s) that hasn't played, as long it's not the last game of the week. In this case, you will score zero points.
Tip: Start all players weekly before the first game starts, then you can make changes prior to each games kickoff.

League Reports:
 All reports are posted at the league's web site. Owners wishing to have certain reports emailed may choose so by checking the option under Owners | Advanced Franchise Setup.

Point distribution:
 Point distributions will be determined by the amount of entries received. The amount for each distribution will be determined after the first round and posted on the league's web site.  In past years, Up to top 5 finishes will be awarded based on owner participation.
